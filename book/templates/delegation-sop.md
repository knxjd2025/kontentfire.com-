## Section 1: Brand Voice

### Where to Find It

Our Brand Voice Profile is saved in the following location(s):

- **Primary location:** [FILL IN - e.g., Google Doc link, shared drive path, printed copy in office binder]
- **Backup location:** [FILL IN - e.g., pinned note in Slack, saved in KontentFire account settings]

### How to Use It

Every time you generate content using an AI tool, you must paste the full Brand Voice Profile at the top of your prompt BEFORE your content request. This ensures the AI writes in our voice, not a generic marketing voice.

**The process:**

1. Open the Brand Voice Profile document.
2. Copy the entire profile (everything between `=== BRAND VOICE PROFILE ===` and `=== END BRAND VOICE PROFILE ===`).
3. Paste it into the AI tool (ChatGPT, Claude, KontentFire, etc.) as the first part of your prompt.
4. After the profile, add your specific content request (e.g., "Now generate 5 Facebook posts about winter pipe protection tips").
5. Review the output against the voice profile. If something sounds off, tell the AI what to fix and regenerate.

### When to Update the Brand Voice Profile

Flag the owner for a Brand Voice update if any of the following happen:

- We earn a new certification, award, or milestone (e.g., hitting 500 Google reviews)
- We add or drop a service
- We expand to a new service area
- We hire or lose team members that changes our team size
- The owner changes how they want the business to sound online

Do NOT update the Brand Voice Profile yourself without owner approval.

## Section 2: Content Calendar

### Weekly Content Themes

Use the following rotating theme schedule for each week. This ensures variety and covers all the content types that drive engagement and leads.

| Day of Week | Theme | Example Post Types |
|---|---|---|
| **Monday** | Motivation / Week Kickoff | Team heading out to jobs, "Here's what we've got on the schedule this week," Monday motivation quotes related to our trade |
| **Tuesday** | Tip Tuesday | Educational tip the homeowner can use, "Did you know..." facts, maintenance reminders |
| **Wednesday** | Work in Progress / Behind the Scenes | Job site photos, process shots, "Here's what we're working on today," time-lapse videos |
| **Thursday** | Throwback / Transformation | Before-and-after photos, "Remember this project from last month?", dramatic reveal posts |
| **Friday** | Fun / Team Culture | Team photos, funny (but professional) moments, weekend prep tips, "Have a great weekend" posts |
| **Saturday** | Social Proof | Customer review spotlight, testimonial graphic, "Thank you to the [last name] family" posts |
| **Sunday** | Rest day OR evergreen content | Optional: reshare a high-performing post from the archive, or schedule nothing |

**Important:** You do NOT need to post every single day. The minimum target is **3 posts per week**. Use the themes above to pick which 3-5 days to cover each week.

## Section 3: The Batching Routine

Content is created in batches, not one post at a time. Follow this routine once per month (or twice per month if preferred).

### Monthly Batching Session

**When:** Schedule a recurring 3-hour block on the calendar. Recommended: first Monday or Tuesday of the month.

**What you'll produce:** 15-25 social media posts for the upcoming month.

### Step-by-Step Process

**Step 1: Gather Raw Material (30 minutes)**

- Pull 5-10 job photos from the past month (ask technicians/crew to send photos weekly -- set a recurring reminder for them)
- Screenshot 3-5 recent Google reviews
- Note any upcoming events, promotions, or seasonal milestones for the month
- Check the seasonal content map above for relevant hooks
- Review last month's top-performing posts (see Section 6) for ideas to repeat or expand

**Step 2: Generate AI Content (60 minutes)**

- Open your AI tool (ChatGPT, Claude, or KontentFire)
- Paste the Brand Voice Profile (see Section 1)
- Generate content in batches by theme:
  - "Generate 4 Tip Tuesday posts about [seasonal topic]"
  - "Write 4 before-and-after captions for these job descriptions: [describe the jobs]"
  - "Create 3 customer review spotlight posts using these reviews: [paste reviews]"
  - "Write 4 behind-the-scenes posts for a [trade] business during [month]"
- Save all generated content in a single document or spreadsheet

**Step 3: Edit Using the Punch List (45 minutes)**

Run every AI-generated post through this Punch List before it goes anywhere:

| Punch List Item | What to Check | Fix If Needed |
|---|---|---|
| **Voice check** | Does this sound like [owner's name] wrote it? | Reword anything that sounds robotic, generic, or too corporate |
| **Accuracy check** | Are all facts, services, and claims correct? | Fix any incorrect information -- AI sometimes makes things up |
| **Local check** | Does it reference our area, community, or local details? | Add a local reference if it's missing |
| **CTA check** | Is there a clear but non-pushy call to action? | Add or soften the call to action as needed |
| **Cringe check** | Would you be embarrassed if a customer read this? | Delete or rewrite anything that feels forced, fake, or cringey |
| **Compliance check** | Does it make any claims that could create legal issues? | Remove guarantees we don't actually offer, results we can't promise |
| **Platform check** | Is the length and format right for the intended platform? | Shorten for Instagram, adjust for Facebook vs. Google Business Profile |
| **Image check** | Is there a photo or graphic to go with this post? | Assign a job photo, create a simple graphic, or note "needs image" |

**Step 4: Organize and Label (15 minutes)**

- Assign each post a publish date based on the weekly theme calendar
- Label each post with the target platform (Facebook, Instagram, Google Business Profile, etc.)
- Note which posts need images and which have images ready
- Flag any posts that need owner review (see Section 4)

**Step 5: Load into Scheduling Tool (30 minutes)**

- Upload all posts to the scheduling tool (see Section 5)
- Attach images to each post
- Set publish dates and times
- Double-check everything one more time before saving

**Total estimated time per monthly batch: 3 hours**

## Section 4: The Editing and Approval Process

### What Requires Owner Approval

The following content types MUST be reviewed and approved by [FILL IN - owner name] before publishing:

- Any post mentioning pricing, discounts, or promotions
- Any post making specific claims about guarantees or warranties
- Any post responding to or referencing a negative review or complaint
- Any post about a new service, new hire, or major business change
- Any post that could be interpreted as a legal claim or promise
- Any post tagging or mentioning another business, partner, or person by name
- The first batch of content (owner should review the first full month to calibrate)

### What Does NOT Require Owner Approval

Once the owner has approved the first month's content and confirmed the voice is right, the following can be published without additional approval:

- Standard educational tips and maintenance reminders
- Before-and-after job photos with standard captions
- Customer review spotlight posts (using real, public reviews)
- Behind-the-scenes and team culture posts
- Seasonal reminders that don't mention pricing
- Reshares or reposts of previously approved content

### Approval Workflow

1. **Team member** completes the monthly content batch and loads it into a shared document or spreadsheet.
2. **Team member** highlights any posts that require owner approval (see list above) in yellow or with a flag.
3. **Team member** sends the document to the owner with the message: "Monthly content batch ready for review. [X] posts flagged for your approval. Please review by [date -- give 3 business days]."
4. **Owner** reviews flagged posts and replies with "Approved," "Approved with changes" (with specific edits), or "Cut this one."
5. **Team member** makes any requested changes, then schedules all approved content.
6. **Turnaround expectation:** Owner reviews within 3 business days. If no response by deadline, team member sends one follow-up reminder. If still no response after 2 more business days, team member publishes all non-flagged content and holds flagged content until approved.

## Section 6: Monthly Metrics Review

### What to Track

Pull these numbers on the first business day of each month for the previous month's performance.

| Metric | Where to Find It | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| **Total posts published** | Scheduling tool dashboard or manual count | Are we hitting our 12-20 posts/month target? |
| **Total reach** | Facebook Insights, Instagram Insights | How many people saw our content? |
| **Total engagement** (likes, comments, shares) | Facebook Insights, Instagram Insights | Are people interacting, or are we talking to ourselves? |
| **Top 3 posts by engagement** | Sort by engagement in platform insights | What content type resonates? Do more of this. |
| **Bottom 3 posts by engagement** | Sort by engagement (lowest) in platform insights | What fell flat? Stop doing this or try a different angle. |
| **New followers/page likes** | Facebook Page Insights, Instagram Profile | Is our audience growing month over month? |
| **Website clicks from social** | Platform insights or Google Analytics (if set up) | Is social driving people to our website or contact page? |
| **Phone calls or messages from social** | Facebook Page inbox, Instagram DMs, call tracking (if available) | The ultimate metric -- is social media generating real leads? |
| **Google Business Profile views** | GBP Insights dashboard | Are more people finding us on Google? |
| **Google Business Profile actions** | GBP Insights (calls, directions, website clicks) | Are GBP posts driving real actions? |

## Section 7: Guardrails

### Content That NEVER Gets Published Without Owner Approval

No matter how experienced you become with this system, the following content types always require the owner to review and explicitly approve before publishing:

1. **Pricing or financial information** -- Any mention of specific prices, rates, discounts, percentage-off deals, financing options, or payment terms
2. **Legal claims** -- Any statement about licensing, insurance, bonding, guarantees, warranties, or certifications that hasn't been pre-approved in the Brand Voice Profile
3. **Negative or defensive content** -- Any response to a bad review, any post that could be read as criticizing a competitor, any content addressing a complaint or controversy
4. **Crisis or sensitive topics** -- Any reference to an accident, injury, property damage, lawsuit, or insurance claim, whether ours or in the industry generally
5. **Political or social commentary** -- Any content that touches on politics, religion, or divisive social issues, even tangentially
6. **Partnerships and endorsements** -- Any post tagging, mentioning, or endorsing another business, product, brand, or individual
7. **New services or capabilities** -- Any post announcing something we haven't publicly offered before
8. **Hiring or staffing** -- Any post about open positions, new hires, or departures (arrivals can be positive, but departures are sensitive)
9. **Customer-identifying content** -- Any post that includes a customer's full name, address, or identifiable property without their explicit written permission (first names and general neighborhoods are usually fine, but ask if unsure)
10. **Anything you're unsure about** -- When in doubt, flag it. It's always better to delay a post by a day than to publish something that causes a problem.

### Content That Must NEVER Be Published, Period

These are absolute rules. No exceptions. No owner override needed because the answer is always no.

- Content that is factually false or makes claims we cannot verify
- Content that reveals confidential customer information
- Content generated by AI that was not reviewed through the Punch List
- Content copied from another business's social media page
- Content that includes profanity, slurs, or crude humor
- Content that could be interpreted as discriminatory toward any group
- Content that shows unsafe work practices (no harness on a roof, no PPE, etc.)
- Content that promises specific results ("We guarantee we'll save you $500" or "Your home value will increase by 20%")
- Content that uses stock photos presented as our own work

### When Something Goes Wrong

If a post generates unexpected negative attention (angry comments, factual complaints, viral for the wrong reasons):

1. **Do not delete the post** unless it contains factually incorrect or harmful information.
2. **Do not respond to negative comments** on the post.
3. **Immediately notify the owner** via phone call or text (not email -- this is time-sensitive).
4. **Screenshot everything** -- the post, all comments, any messages received.
5. **Wait for owner direction** before taking any action.
