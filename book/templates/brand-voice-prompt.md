# Brand Voice Template

## How to Use This Template

This template helps you define your brand's unique voice so that any AI tool (ChatGPT, Claude, KontentFire, etc.) can generate social media content that sounds like YOU -- not like a generic marketing robot.

**Instructions:**

1. Read through each section below.
2. Replace every `[FILL IN]` marker with your specific information.
3. Be honest and specific. The more detail you give, the better your AI-generated content will sound.
4. Once completed, save this as a note on your phone or a document on your computer.
5. Every time you use an AI tool to generate content, paste this entire completed template at the top of your prompt before your request.

**Time to complete:** 15-20 minutes

**Tip:** If you get stuck on a section, ask yourself: "How would I explain this to a new customer standing in their driveway?" That's your voice.

## Example 1: Completed Template for a Plumbing Company

```
=== BRAND VOICE PROFILE ===

BUSINESS BASICS
- Business name: FlowRight Plumbing
- Trade/service type: Residential and light commercial plumbing
- Location/service area: Greater Nashville, TN area including Franklin, Brentwood, Murfreesboro, and Hendersonville
- Years in business: 14
- Team size: Me (owner/lead plumber) and 3 service techs
- What makes you different from competitors: We answer the phone live, every time -- no phone trees, no voicemail during business hours. We also do flat-rate pricing so there are never surprises on the bill.

TARGET CUSTOMER
- Who is your ideal customer: Homeowners aged 30-65 in the Nashville suburbs who want reliable, honest plumbing work without feeling like they're being taken for a ride
- What problem do they usually call you about first: A water heater that's not heating, a toilet that won't stop running, or a slow drain that's getting worse
- What are they most afraid of when hiring someone in your trade: Getting a lowball estimate and then hit with hidden charges, or having someone cut corners behind the walls where they can't see

VOICE & PERSONALITY
- If your business were a person at a BBQ, how would they talk: Like your neighbor who happens to be a plumber. Easy to talk to, gives you a straight answer, and might crack a joke about how many times he's been asked to "just take a quick look" at someone's toilet at a party.
- Words/phrases you actually use when talking to customers: "Here's what I found," "Let me show you," "No surprises on the bill," "We'll get you taken care of," "That's a smart question," "I'd do the same thing in your house"
- Words/phrases you would NEVER use: Synergy, optimize, leverage, "act now before it's too late," anything that sounds like a used car commercial
- Your tone is: Friendly and knowledgeable, confident without being arrogant, plain-spoken
- Level of humor: Light humor, self-deprecating plumber jokes are fine, nothing crude or political

CONTENT PREFERENCES
- Types of posts that feel right for your brand: Before-and-after photos of jobs, quick plumbing tips homeowners can do themselves, seasonal reminders (winterizing pipes, water heater maintenance), team spotlights, photos from community events
- Types of posts that feel wrong for your brand: Anything bashing competitors, overly technical jargon, high-pressure sales posts, anything political or controversial
- Call-to-action style: Helpful and low-pressure -- "If your water heater is over 10 years old, it might be time for a checkup. Give us a call and we'll take a look" rather than "CALL NOW! LIMITED TIME OFFER!"
- Hashtag preference: 3-5 max, always include #NashvillePlumber and #NashvilleTN

TRUST SIGNALS TO MENTION
- Licenses/certifications: Tennessee State Licensed Master Plumber, fully insured, backflow certified
- Awards or recognitions: Best of Nashville Scene 2023 and 2024, Google Guaranteed
- Guarantees you offer: Flat-rate pricing guarantee, 1-year warranty on all labor, same-day service for emergencies
- Number of reviews/rating: 340+ five-star reviews on Google, 4.9 average

SEASONAL/LOCAL CONTEXT
- Busy season(s): Winter is biggest for burst pipes and water heater failures, spring for sump pump work after rain season
- Local references to include: Reference neighborhoods like The Gulch, East Nashville, Brentwood, and Franklin. We sponsor a local Habitat for Humanity build every year.
- Weather/climate factors: Cold snaps in January/February cause pipe freezes, spring storms bring flooding and sump pump overloads, summer humidity means basement moisture issues

=== END BRAND VOICE PROFILE ===
```

## Example 2: Completed Template for a Landscaping Company

```
=== BRAND VOICE PROFILE ===

BUSINESS BASICS
- Business name: GreenStone Landscapes
- Trade/service type: Full-service residential landscaping -- design, installation, maintenance, and hardscaping
- Location/service area: Scottsdale, Paradise Valley, and North Phoenix, AZ
- Years in business: 8
- Team size: 2 design/sales, 14-person field crew across 3 teams
- What makes you different from competitors: We specialize in desert-adapted landscaping that actually looks lush and green without wasting water. We also do full 3D design renderings before any work starts so you see exactly what you're getting.

TARGET CUSTOMER
- Who is your ideal customer: Homeowners in the $500K-$2M home range who want their outdoor space to look amazing and work for the desert climate. Many are transplants from wetter states who don't know what's possible in Arizona.
- What problem do they usually call you about first: Their yard looks like a gravel pit and they want a real outdoor living space, or their existing landscaping is dying because it wasn't designed for the climate
- What are they most afraid of when hiring someone in your trade: Spending $20K+ and having plants die in six months, or getting a result that looks nothing like what they imagined

VOICE & PERSONALITY
- If your business were a person at a BBQ, how would they talk: The person who walks into your backyard and immediately starts pointing out what's possible -- "You could put a fire pit right there, some agave along that wall, and a pergola over the patio. This space has so much potential." Enthusiastic, visual, inspiring.
- Words/phrases you actually use when talking to customers: "Let me show you what this could look like," "Desert doesn't mean boring," "This is built to thrive here," "Imagine sitting out here on a fall evening," "Low water doesn't mean low impact"
- Words/phrases you would NEVER use: Cheap, discount, budget, basic, cookie-cutter, one-size-fits-all
- Your tone is: Enthusiastic and inspiring, knowledgeable about desert horticulture, premium but approachable
- Level of humor: Occasional light humor, especially about Arizona heat -- "Yes, we work in 115 degrees. No, we don't recommend it."

CONTENT PREFERENCES
- Types of posts that feel right for your brand: Dramatic before-and-after transformations, 3D renderings next to finished results, plant spotlights (desert species people don't know about), outdoor living inspiration, time-lapse install videos
- Types of posts that feel wrong for your brand: Anything that looks cheap or discount-focused, stock photos, generic gardening tips that don't apply to the desert, posts about mowing lawns (we don't do standard lawn care)
- Call-to-action style: Inspirational -- "Ready to turn your yard into your favorite room? Let's start with a free design consultation" rather than "Call today for a free estimate!"
- Hashtag preference: 4-6 hashtags, always include #ScottsdaleLandscaping #DesertLandscape #ArizonaOutdoorLiving

TRUST SIGNALS TO MENTION
- Licenses/certifications: Arizona ROC licensed, certified irrigation contractor, NALP certified landscape professional
- Awards or recognitions: Houzz Design Award 2024, featured in Phoenix Home & Garden magazine twice
- Guarantees you offer: 1-year plant replacement guarantee on all installations, design satisfaction guarantee (we revise until you love it)
- Number of reviews/rating: 180+ five-star reviews on Google, 4.9 on Houzz

SEASONAL/LOCAL CONTEXT
- Busy season(s): October through April is prime install season, summer is maintenance and irrigation work
- Local references to include: Reference Scottsdale, Paradise Valley, Carefree, DC Ranch, Grayhawk, McDowell Mountain communities. We've done work for several Scottsdale HOAs.
- Weather/climate factors: Extreme summer heat (115F+) limits what can be planted and when, monsoon season in July-August brings flash floods and erosion concerns, mild winters are the perfect time for outdoor entertaining

=== END BRAND VOICE PROFILE ===
```

## Example 3: Completed Template for an HVAC Company

```
=== BRAND VOICE PROFILE ===

BUSINESS BASICS
- Business name: ComfortPro Heating & Cooling
- Trade/service type: Residential HVAC -- installation, repair, and maintenance for heating and air conditioning systems
- Location/service area: Columbus, OH and surrounding suburbs including Dublin, Westerville, Gahanna, and Reynoldsburg
- Years in business: 22
- Team size: 3 office staff, 8 service technicians, 2 install crews
- What makes you different from competitors: We've been family-owned for 22 years and most of our techs have been with us for 5+ years. We also offer a maintenance membership that includes priority scheduling and 15% off repairs -- most of our customers are members.

TARGET CUSTOMER
- Who is your ideal customer: Homeowners who value comfort and reliability, typically aged 35-70, who'd rather invest in quality HVAC work than deal with the cheapest option breaking down in two years
- What problem do they usually call you about first: AC not cooling in the summer or furnace not heating in the winter -- usually something that's been "acting up" for a while before it finally dies
- What are they most afraid of when hiring someone in your trade: Being told they need a full system replacement when they only need a repair, or paying for a repair that doesn't actually fix the problem

VOICE & PERSONALITY
- If your business were a person at a BBQ, how would they talk: Like your uncle who's been in the business for decades. Calm, reassuring, tells it to you straight. Doesn't rush you into a decision. Might say, "Look, you've got some life left in that unit, but here's what I'd plan for in the next year or two."
- Words/phrases you actually use when talking to customers: "Let me walk you through your options," "Here's what I'd recommend if this were my house," "Your system is telling us something," "We'll get your comfort back," "No pressure, just honest advice"
- Words/phrases you would NEVER use: Cutting-edge, revolutionary, game-changing, URGENT, limited time only, act now
- Your tone is: Calm and reassuring, honest and straightforward, experienced and trustworthy
- Level of humor: Minimal -- warm and personable but more reassuring than funny. The occasional "We love a good thermostat war story" is about as humorous as we get.

CONTENT PREFERENCES
- Types of posts that feel right for your brand: Seasonal maintenance reminders, energy-saving tips, "what that sound means" educational posts, team member spotlights, membership program benefits, new system installations
- Types of posts that feel wrong for your brand: Fear-mongering ("Your furnace could EXPLODE!"), overly casual or meme-heavy content, anything that feels like a high-pressure sales tactic, competitor bashing
- Call-to-action style: Warm and helpful -- "Fall is the perfect time to schedule your furnace tune-up. Give us a call and we'll make sure you're set for winter" rather than "BOOK NOW -- SLOTS FILLING FAST!"
- Hashtag preference: 3-4 max, always include #ColumbusOhio and #HVAC, keep them professional

TRUST SIGNALS TO MENTION
- Licenses/certifications: Ohio HVAC contractor license, NATE-certified technicians, EPA Section 608 certified, Carrier Factory Authorized Dealer
- Awards or recognitions: Angi Super Service Award 5 years running, BBB A+ rating, Carrier President's Award
- Guarantees you offer: 100% satisfaction guarantee on all repairs, 10-year parts and labor warranty on new installations, 24/7 emergency service for members
- Number of reviews/rating: 520+ reviews on Google with a 4.8 average

SEASONAL/LOCAL CONTEXT
- Busy season(s): June through August is peak AC season, November through February is peak heating season. Spring and fall are ideal for installations and maintenance.
- Local references to include: Reference OSU game days (thermostats cranked up for watch parties), Dublin Irish Festival, Columbus neighborhoods like German Village, Short North, Upper Arlington. We donate to the Columbus Foundation annually.
- Weather/climate factors: Hot, humid summers (90F+ with high humidity makes AC essential), cold winters with ice and snow (furnace failures are emergencies), spring brings allergy season (air quality/filter posts), unpredictable Ohio weather means "four seasons in one week" jokes resonate

=== END BRAND VOICE PROFILE ===
```

## Next Steps

Once you've completed your Brand Voice Template:

1. **Save it** somewhere easy to access (phone notes, Google Doc, or a printed copy in your office).
2. **Paste it** at the beginning of every AI prompt you write for social media content.
3. **Update it** every 6 months or whenever something significant changes about your business.
4. **Share it** with anyone who creates content for your business -- employees, virtual assistants, marketing help.

Your Brand Voice Template is the foundation everything else in this book builds on. Get this right, and your AI-generated content will sound like it came from you on your best day.
