# Platform Tone Adapter Prompts

**The AI Apprentice: How Trade Service Businesses Build a Content Engine That Prints Money**

> These prompts take any piece of content you have already written (or generated with AI) and reformat it for a specific social media platform. Paste your content where it says `[ORIGINAL CONTENT]` and let AI handle the platform-specific adaptation.

---

## How to Use These Adapters

1. **Create your base content first.** Use a prompt from the Trade Prompt Library or write something from scratch.
2. **Choose your platform adapter.** Copy the adapter prompt for your target platform.
3. **Paste your content into the placeholder.** Replace `[ORIGINAL CONTENT]` with the content you want to adapt.
4. **Generate and review.** The AI will reformat your content to fit that platform's style, length, and audience expectations.

You can run the same piece of content through multiple adapters to create platform-specific versions from a single idea.

## Platform Adapter Prompts

### 1. Facebook Adapter

```
You are a social media expert who specializes in Facebook content for local service businesses. Take the following content and rewrite it specifically for Facebook.

Follow these Facebook-specific rules:
- Lead with a conversational hook or a relatable story. Facebook rewards posts that feel like a friend talking, not a business broadcasting.
- Write in a warm, community-oriented tone. Use "you" and "your" frequently.
- Aim for 100-200 words. Facebook users will read longer posts IF the first two lines hook them. Front-load the most interesting or useful part.
- Break the text into short paragraphs (2-3 sentences max) for easy mobile reading.
- Include a clear call to action at the end — but make it feel like a helpful suggestion, not a sales pitch.
- Do NOT include hashtags. They do not help on Facebook and look spammy.
- If the content is educational, frame it as "Here is something most homeowners in [CITY] do not know..."
- If the content is promotional, frame it as a favor: "We wanted to let our [CITY] neighbors know about this before we book up..."
- Add a question at the end to encourage comments (Facebook's algorithm rewards engagement).

Here is the content to adapt:

[ORIGINAL CONTENT]
```

### 2. Instagram Caption Adapter

```
You are a social media expert who specializes in Instagram content for local service businesses. Take the following content and rewrite it as an Instagram caption.

Follow these Instagram-specific rules:
- Start with a strong hook in the first line — this is the only line visible before "...more." Make it impossible not to tap.
- Keep the main caption between 75-150 words. Concise and punchy.
- Use short, scannable paragraphs or line breaks between thoughts.
- Write as if narrating a photo or video. Reference what the viewer is seeing: "This is what 20 years of neglect looks like..." or "Swipe to see the transformation."
- Include a clear call to action (DM us, tap the link in bio, save this for later).
- Add a line break after the caption, then include 15-20 relevant hashtags. Mix these three types:
  - 5-7 broad industry hashtags (e.g., #PlumbingLife, #HomeRepair)
  - 5-7 local hashtags (e.g., #[CITY]Plumber, #[CITY]HomeServices)
  - 3-5 niche/specific hashtags (e.g., #WaterHeaterReplacement, #LeakRepair)
- Use 2-4 emojis naturally within the caption — not at the start of every line.
- Tone should be confident, visual, and slightly more polished than Facebook.

Here is the content to adapt:

[ORIGINAL CONTENT]
```

### 3. TikTok Caption Adapter

```
You are a social media expert who specializes in TikTok content for local service businesses. Take the following content and rewrite it as a TikTok caption.

Follow these TikTok-specific rules:
- The caption must be under 50 words. On TikTok, the video does the talking — the caption just hooks people into watching.
- Lead with a curiosity-driven or emotional hook. Examples: "This is why your electric bill is so high." or "POV: You call us at 2 AM and we actually show up."
- Use casual, conversational language. Write like you talk. Contractions, slang, and sentence fragments are fine.
- Do NOT include a long call to action. One simple line at most: "Follow for more" or "Link in bio."
- Include 3-5 hashtags max, focused on discoverability:
  - 1-2 trending or broad hashtags (#HomeImprovement, #LearnOnTikTok)
  - 1-2 trade-specific hashtags (#PlumberLife, #RoofingTikTok)
  - 1 local hashtag (#[CITY])
- If the content is educational, frame the caption as a question or bold claim: "Stop doing this to your AC" or "3 things your contractor will never tell you."
- If the content is a before/after, the caption should tease the result: "Wait for it..." or "The homeowner's reaction at the end."
- Tone should be raw, authentic, and high-energy.

Here is the content to adapt:

[ORIGINAL CONTENT]
```

### 5. LinkedIn Adapter

```
You are a social media expert who specializes in LinkedIn content for service business owners. Take the following content and rewrite it for LinkedIn.

Follow these LinkedIn-specific rules:
- Reframe the content from a business owner or industry professional perspective. LinkedIn is B2B and thought-leadership oriented.
- Lead with a hook that speaks to other business owners, property managers, general contractors, or commercial clients — not residential homeowners.
- Write 150-250 words. LinkedIn rewards longer, thoughtful posts that demonstrate expertise.
- Use a professional tone but keep it human and story-driven. Share a lesson learned, an industry insight, or a behind-the-scenes look at running a trade business.
- Structure with short paragraphs and line breaks for readability.
- If the content is educational, position it as industry expertise: "After 15 years in the trade, here is what most people get wrong about..."
- If the content is a before/after, frame it as a case study: the problem, the approach, the result, and the takeaway for other professionals.
- End with a question or invitation that encourages professional engagement: "What is the most common issue you see in your field?" or "If you manage properties in [CITY], let's connect."
- Include 3-5 professional hashtags at the end: #[Trade]Industry, #SmallBusiness, #[CITY]Business, #TradeLife, #ServiceBusiness
- Do NOT use consumer-facing language ("Book now!", "Call today!"). Instead use "Let's connect" or "Happy to discuss."
```

## Video Script Prompts (Chapter 5)

These prompts generate short-form video scripts designed for TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts, and Facebook Reels. Each follows a proven format that performs well for trade service businesses.

### 1. The "Before & After Reveal" (30 Seconds)

```
You are a video content strategist for trade service businesses. Write a 30-second video script using the "Before & After Reveal" format.

Trade: [TRADE]
Scene description: [DESCRIBE THE BEFORE SCENE - what does the problem look like?]
Specific detail: [SPECIFIC DETAIL about what was wrong or what you found]

Script structure:
- HOOK (0-3 seconds): Open with a bold statement or question that stops the scroll. Example: "This is the worst [problem] I have seen in 20 years." Show the ugly "before" immediately.
- PROBLEM (3-10 seconds): Narrate what the viewer is seeing. Be specific — what is wrong, how bad it is, and what would happen if it were ignored. Show close-ups of the damage or issue.
- TRANSITION (10-12 seconds): Quick cut or transition effect. Say something like "But watch this..." or "Here is what it looks like now." Build anticipation.
- REVEAL (12-25 seconds): Show the completed work in a slow pan or wide shot. Narrate the result: what was done, how long it took, and the homeowner's reaction if available.
- CTA (25-30 seconds): End card or spoken CTA. "[BUSINESS NAME] in [CITY]. Link in bio for a free estimate."

Include notes for:
- Camera angles for the before shots (close-ups of damage)
- Transition style (swipe, hard cut, or time-lapse)
- Music suggestion (trending audio or genre)
- On-screen text overlays at key moments
```

### 2. The "Quick Tip from the Truck" (15 Seconds)

```
You are a video content strategist for trade service businesses. Write a 15-second video script using the "Quick Tip from the Truck" format. This is filmed by the technician sitting in or standing by their work truck, talking directly to camera.

Trade: [TRADE]
Specific tip topic: [DESCRIBE THE TIP - what common mistake or useful advice?]

Script structure:
- HOOK (0-3 seconds): "Here is something most homeowners do not know about their [system/feature]." The technician is in the truck or leaning against it. Casual, unpolished, real.
- TIP (3-12 seconds): Deliver one specific, actionable tip in plain language. No jargon. The viewer should be able to act on this today. If possible, hold up a prop (a dirty filter, a part, a tool) to make it visual.
- TAG (12-15 seconds): "Follow for more tips from a real [TRADE PROFESSIONAL] in [CITY]." Smile. Done.

Include notes for:
- Framing (vertical, head and shoulders, truck visible in background for credibility)
- Lighting (natural daylight works best — film before heading to the next job)
- Prop suggestions specific to the tip
- On-screen text: display the tip as text overlay for viewers watching without sound
- Why this format works: it builds trust, takes 2 minutes to film, and performs well because of its authenticity
```

### 3. The "You Won't Believe This" Discovery (30 Seconds)

```
You are a video content strategist for trade service businesses. Write a 30-second video script using the "You Won't Believe What We Found" format. This is filmed on a job site when the technician discovers something unexpected, shocking, or interesting.

Trade: [TRADE]
Discovery scene: [DESCRIBE WHAT WAS FOUND - the unexpected thing behind the wall, under the floor, on the roof, etc.]
Specific detail: [SPECIFIC DETAIL about why this is surprising or dangerous]

Script structure:
- HOOK (0-3 seconds): Start mid-action. The technician looks at the camera with genuine reaction: "You are NOT going to believe what we just found behind this [wall/ceiling/panel]." Voice should convey real surprise or concern.
- REVEAL (3-12 seconds): Slowly show the discovery. Let the camera linger. The technician narrates what they are seeing and why it is a big deal. Use specific trade language but explain it: "See these black marks? That is mold from a leak that has been going on for years."
- CONTEXT (12-22 seconds): Explain the implications. How dangerous is it? How much would it cost if they had not caught it? How common is this in homes in [CITY]? Make the viewer think about their own home.
- CTA (22-30 seconds): "This is why regular [inspections/maintenance] matter. If you are in [CITY] and you have not had a [TRADE] inspection in a while, hit the link in our bio. [BUSINESS NAME] — we find problems before they find you."

Include notes for:
- Camera work (shaky, handheld feel adds authenticity — do NOT over-produce this)
- Reaction shot (genuine facial expression matters — film the real reaction if possible)
- Lighting (use a flashlight or work light pointed at the discovery for dramatic effect)
- On-screen text overlays explaining what the viewer is looking at
- Sound: original audio preferred over music for this format (the reaction is the content)
```

### 4. The "Why You Should Hire a Pro" Educational (45 Seconds)

```
You are a video content strategist for trade service businesses. Write a 45-second video script using the "Why You Should Hire a Pro" format. This is an educational video that shows why a specific job should not be DIY.

Trade: [TRADE]
Job being discussed: [DESCRIBE THE JOB that homeowners sometimes try to DIY]
Specific detail: [SPECIFIC DETAIL about what goes wrong when it is done incorrectly]

Script structure:
- HOOK (0-5 seconds): "I see homeowners try to [DIY TASK] all the time. Here is why that is a terrible idea." Cut to footage of a botched DIY job (real example from a call if possible, or staged recreation).
- PROBLEM 1 (5-15 seconds): Show and explain the first thing that goes wrong with DIY. Be specific and visual. "See how this is connected? That is not to code and it is a [fire hazard/flood risk/safety issue]."
- PROBLEM 2 (15-25 seconds): Show a second common DIY mistake. "And this right here? This is what happens when you use [wrong material/technique]. It worked for six months, then [consequence]."
- PRO DIFFERENCE (25-38 seconds): Show the professional way. Side-by-side if possible. "When we do this job, here is how it looks. [Explain the proper technique, materials, and why it matters.] This will last [YEARS] instead of [MONTHS]."
- CTA (38-45 seconds): "Some jobs are worth doing yourself. This is not one of them. [BUSINESS NAME] in [CITY] — call us before you call YouTube." End with a smile or humor.

Include notes for:
- Split-screen or side-by-side comparison shots (DIY vs. professional)
- Close-up shots of the specific mistakes and proper techniques
- On-screen text labels: "DIY" vs. "Professional" or "Wrong" vs. "Right"
- Tone: educational, not condescending — the goal is to earn trust, not mock people
- Music: subtle background track, voice is the star
```

### 5. The "Meet the Team" Introduction (30 Seconds)

```
You are a video content strategist for trade service businesses. Write a 30-second video script using the "Meet the Team" format. This introduces one team member in a way that builds trust and humanizes the business.

Trade: [TRADE]
Team member details: [DESCRIBE THE PERSON - their name, role, years of experience, one personal detail like a hobby or family]

Script structure:
- INTRO (0-5 seconds): Open on the team member at work or in front of the truck. They introduce themselves naturally: "Hey, I am [NAME]. I am a [ROLE] at [BUSINESS NAME] and I have been doing this for [YEARS] years."
- WHAT THEY DO (5-15 seconds): Quick montage or continued shot of them working. They describe what a typical day looks like in one or two sentences. Make it specific and visual: "Today I am replacing a [SPECIFIC THING] for a family in [CITY NEIGHBORHOOD]. It is a [DURATION] job and it is one of my favorites."
- PERSONAL TOUCH (15-22 seconds): One human detail that makes them relatable. "When I am not on the job, I am [HOBBY/FAMILY DETAIL]. But honestly, I love what I do — there is nothing better than fixing a problem and seeing the relief on a homeowner's face."
- CLOSE (22-30 seconds): "If you see this truck in your [CITY] neighborhood, come say hi. Or better yet, give us a call if you need anything. [BUSINESS NAME] — real people, real work." Wave or thumbs up.

Include notes for:
- Filming location (on a job site or by the branded truck for credibility)
- Wardrobe (company uniform or branded shirt — reinforces professionalism)
- Framing (vertical for Reels/TikTok, keep it casual and warm)
- Music: light, upbeat background track — not overpowering
- On-screen text: name, role, and years of experience as an overlay
- Why this works: people hire people, not logos. This format builds the "know, like, trust" factor faster than any ad.
```

## Adapter Prompt Tips

### Repurposing Workflow

The most efficient content workflow for trade service businesses:

1. **Write one strong base piece of content** using a prompt from the Trade Prompt Library.
2. **Run it through the Facebook Adapter** first (Facebook is the most forgiving platform and the longest format).
3. **Adapt to Instagram** by tightening the copy and adding visual references and hashtags.
4. **Adapt to TikTok** by stripping it down to the hook and one key point.
5. **Adapt to Google Business Profile** by making it professional and local-SEO-focused.
6. **Adapt to LinkedIn** only if you target commercial clients or want referral partnerships.

One idea becomes five pieces of content in under 15 minutes.

### When to Use the Universal Multi-Platform Prompt

Use the Universal prompt when:
- You are short on time and want all platforms done at once
- The content idea is simple and straightforward
- You are batch-creating a week of content in one sitting

Use individual adapters when:
- The content is complex or nuanced
- You want more control over each platform's version
- You are creating a high-priority post (big promo, announcement, viral attempt)

### Video Script Selection Guide

| Format | Best For | Difficulty | Equipment Needed |
|--------|----------|------------|-----------------|
| Before & After Reveal | Showcasing completed work | Easy | Phone camera |
| Quick Tip from the Truck | Building authority fast | Easiest | Phone only |
| You Won't Believe This | Going viral, shock value | Medium | Phone + flashlight |
| Why Hire a Pro | Education + trust | Medium | Phone, examples of DIY fails |
| Meet the Team | Humanizing the business | Easy | Phone, company uniform |

Start with "Quick Tip from the Truck" — it takes 2 minutes to film, requires zero editing, and consistently outperforms polished content because it feels real.
